I am a first-year PhD student in the Department of Statistics at the University of Washington, Seattle.

Currently, I am working with Peter Guttorp on problems arising in statistical climatology, most prominently the statistical modeling of cloud columns and uncertainty assessment for use in general circulation models.

I received my B.S. and M.S. degrees in Applied Mathematics from the University of Colorado at Boulder, under the supervision of Will Kleiber. My thesis introduced approximate Bayesian computation techniques for daily spatiotemporal temperature and precipitation occurrence simulation.
