Branden Olson

Research

Broadly speaking, my skills and interests lie at the interface of statistics and computer science.

More specifically, my research interests include spatiotemporal statistics and its application to climatology and meteorology. I develop stochastic weather generators (SWGs) and implement them using fast Markov chain Monte Carlo approachces. I've also taken an interest in contemporary Bayesian methods, particularly approximate Bayesian computation (ABC) techniques for problems with intractable likelihoods.

In the past I have also worked on problems in dynamical systems, such as investigating the effect of inhibitory neurons in artifical neural networks.
