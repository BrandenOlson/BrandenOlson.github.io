Research

Broadly speaking, my interests lie at the interface of statistics and computer science with an emphasis on stochastic modeling.

More specifically, my research involves spatiotemporal statistics and its application to climatology and meteorology, such as developing stochastic weather generators (SWGs) and implementing them using fast Markov chain Monte Carlo approaches. I've also taken an interest in contemporary Bayesian methods, particularly approximate Bayesian computation (ABC) techniques for problems with unknown or intractable likelihoods.

In the past I have also worked on problems in dynamical systems, such as investigating the effect of inhibitory neurons in artifical neural networks.

Other fields of interest include statistical machine learning, mathematical finance, and data visualization.
